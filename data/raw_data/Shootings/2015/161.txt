Baltimore police on Wednesday said they are investigating shootings that injured eight people, including a quadruple shooting that left a 16-year-old dead, since Tuesday night  Police said the quadruple shooting occurred in the 3500 block of Garrison Blvd, near Liberty Heights Ave., in the Dorchester neighborhood on the city's west side. Officers first learned of the shooting after they were called to a local hospital at 1:12 a.m. when staff notified police of the four gunshot victims that had arrived on their own accord.  The 16-year-old died after he was shot in the chest, police said. He has not yet been publicly identified. In addition to the teen, a 19-year old man was shot in the hand, a 21-year-old man was shot in the arm, and a 22-year-old man suffered a graze to the head in the incident.  Homicide detectives are investigating and ask anyone with information to call 410-396-2100.  The department has reported 168 homicides so far this year, up from 106 at the same time last year. Twenty-four people have been killed in Baltimore this month.
