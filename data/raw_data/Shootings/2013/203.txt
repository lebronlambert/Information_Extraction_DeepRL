An arrest was made in a shooting last week that left four people injured and one woman dead, police announced Tuesday.  A 27-year-old Detroit man was arrested Friday in connection with the shooting, which happened on May 15, Detroit Police Sgt. Eren Stephens said. She said the Wayne County Prosecutor's Office is reviewing charges.  Police said a 54-year-old woman was killed in the shooting on Carlin, near Orangelawn, on the city's west side. A 58-year-old man, 32-year-old woman, 24-year-old woman and a 15-year-old girl were wounded in the shooting, according to police.  Stephens said police are still looking for a second person they believe was involved in the shooting
