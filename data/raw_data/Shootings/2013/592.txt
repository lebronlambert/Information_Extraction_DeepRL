Multiple people were wounded in a shooting in Northeast Washington D.C. on Wednesday afternoon.  UPDATE: Victor Leon Coley has been charged in the shooting, according to The Washington Post.  D.C. Police, Fire and Emergency Services responded to the scene at the 3900 block of Minnesota Ave. around 1:20 p.m., according to NBC Washington.  Reports indicate that four people were shot, with two in serious but stable condition. The other two victims suffered non-life threatening injuries, including a 60-year-old woman who was shot in the knee.  While no one has yet been charged in the crime, police say they have two people of interest currently in custody. There is no word on a possible motive.  Officials told WTOP that the shooting does not appear to be random.    This is a developing story. Please check back for updates.
